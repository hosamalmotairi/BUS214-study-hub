Chapter 5 — Recognizing Ethical Issues in Business

الفصل الخامس — التعرف على القضايا الأخلاقية في الأعمال

Source: Ferrell, Business Ethics 13e · Ch. 5

🎯 Key Takeaways — أهم النقاط (ملخّص سريع)
  • Ethical Issue: choice among right/wrong actions. Dilemma: all alternatives negative.قضية (اختر صح/غلط) — معضلة (كل الخيارات سلبية)
  • 3 Foundational Values: Integrity · Honesty · Fairness. 3 قيم أساسية: نزاهة، أمانة، إنصاف.
  • Fairness has 3 elements: Equality · Reciprocity · Optimization.الإنصاف: مساواة، معاملة بالمثل، تحسين
  • Lying: Commission (deceiving words) vs Omission (not telling).كذب بالفعل vs كذب بالإغفال
  • Bribery: Active=giver, Passive=receiver. Facilitation payments legal in US (FCPA), illegal in UK.رشوة نشطة/سلبية — مدفوعات التسهيل
  • Hostile Work Environment: Unwelcome · Severe/Pervasive · Reasonable Person.3 معايير للبيئة العدائية
  • Insider Trading legal ONLY if in own company AND reported to SEC within 2 business days.تداول داخلي قانوني بشرط: شركتك + إبلاغ SEC خلال يومين
  • Consumer Fraud: Collusion (employee helps), Duplicity (fake accident), Guile (tricks).احتيال المستهلك: 3 أنواع
  • FCPA = US law banning bribery of foreign officials.FCPA = قانون منع رشوة المسؤولين الأجانب
  • Age Discrimination Act protects 40 and older; retirement before 70 cannot be forced.ADEA: حماية 40+، ومنع إلزام التقاعد قبل 70

5-1 Recognizing an Ethical Issue التعرف على القضية الأخلاقية

  • People make ethical decisions when they find an ethical component in a particular issue or situation.يتخذ الأفراد قرارات أخلاقية عندما يجدون بُعداً أخلاقياً في قضية أو موقف معينKey Word: Ethical Component
  • Failure to acknowledge or be aware of ethical issues is hazardous to an organization.يُعدّ تجاهل القضايا الأخلاقية أو عدم الوعي بها أمراً خطيراً على أي منظمةKey Word: Awareness Matters
  • In a dilemma, all of the alternatives have negative consequences, so the less harmful choice is made.في حالة المعضلة، تنطوي جميع البدائل على عواقب سلبية، لذا يُختار الخيار الأقل ضرراًKey Word: All Choices Negative
  • Collusion — A secret agreement between two or more parties for a fraudulent, illegal, or deceitful purpose.التواطؤ: اتفاق سري بين طرفين أو أكثر لغرض احتيالي أو غير قانوني أو خادعKey Word: Secret Fraud Pact
  • Deceitful purpose — Suggests trickery, misrepresentation, or a strategy designed to lead others to believe something less than the whole truth.الغرض الخادع: خداع أو تضليل الآخرين وإيهامهم بجزء من الحقيقةKey Word: Less Than Whole Truth

Foundational Values for Identifying Ethical Issues القيم الأساسية لتحديد القضايا الأخلاقية

  • Integrity — One of the most important elements of virtue; refers to being whole, sound, and in an unimpaired condition. Implies a balanced organization that makes ethical financial decisions and is ethical in the more subjective aspects of its corporate culture.النزاهة: أحد أهم عناصر الفضيلة؛ تعني منظمة متوازنة أخلاقية في قراراتها المالية وفي ثقافتها المؤسسيةKey Word: Whole & Sound
  • Honesty — Refers to truthfulness or trustworthiness.الأمانة: الصدق أو الجدارة بالثقةKey Word: Truthfulness
  • Dishonesty — A lack or absence of integrity, incomplete disclosure, and an unwillingness to tell the truth.الخداع: انعدام النزاهة، وعدم الإفصاح الكامل، وعدم الرغبة في قول الحقيقةKey Word: Absence of Integrity
  • Fairness — The quality of being just, equitable, and impartial. Overlaps with concepts of justice, equity, and equality.الإنصاف: صفة العدل والإنصاف والحيادKey Word: Just & Impartial
Three Elements Motivating Fairness:
  1. Equality — Fair and even distribution of benefits and resources.المساواة: التوزيع العادل للمنافع والموارد
  2. Reciprocity — An interchange of giving and receiving in social relationships.المعاملة بالمثل: تبادل العطاء والأخذ في العلاقات الاجتماعية
  3. Optimization — The trade-off between equity (equality) and efficiency (maximum productivity).التحسين الأمثل: المفاضلة بين الإنصاف والكفاءة

5-2 Emerging Ethical Issues and Dilemmas القضايا والمعضلات الأخلاقية الناشئة

  • Ethical issue — A problem, situation, or opportunity that requires an individual, group, or organization to choose among several actions that must be evaluated as right or wrong, ethical or unethical.المسألة الأخلاقية: موقف يتطلب الاختيار بين عدة خيارات تُقيَّم على أنها صواب أو خطأKey Word: Right vs Wrong Choice
  • Ethical dilemma — A problem that requires choosing among several actions that have negative outcomes. There is not a right choice — only less unethical or illegal choices.المعضلة الأخلاقية: الاختيار بين خيارات كلها نتائج سلبية — لا يوجد خيار صحيح مطلقKey Word: All Negative Outcomes

1) Misuse of Company Time and Resources إساءة استخدام وقت وموارد الشركة

  • Time theft costs companies hundreds of billions of dollars annually.إهدار الوقت يكلف الشركات مئات المليارات سنوياًKey Word: Time Theft
  • Examples: late arrivals, leaving early, long lunches, inappropriate sick days, excessive socializing, personal activities during work hours.التأخر، المغادرة المبكرة، الاستراحات الطويلة، الإجازات غير المبررة، الاختلاط المفرط، الأنشطة الشخصية
  • Using company computers and internet for personal business is one of the most common misuses.استخدام حواسيب وإنترنت الشركة لأغراض شخصية من أكثر أشكال الإساءة شيوعاً

2) Abusive or Intimidating Behavior السلوك المسيء أو الترهيبي

  • A common ethical problem that may include: physical threats, false accusations, being annoying, profanity, insults, yelling, harshness, ignoring someone, unreasonableness.التهديدات الجسدية، الاتهامات الباطلة، الإزعاج، الألفاظ النابية، الإهانات، الصراخ، القسوة، التجاهل، التصرفات غير المنطقيةKey Word: Intent Matters
  • Bullying creates a hostile work environment.يخلق التنمر بيئة عمل عدائية
Note: Within abusive behavior or intimidation, intent should be a consideration. Employers should create policies with reprimand letters/dismissal, emphasize mutual respect, and encourage reporting via hotlines.

3) Lying الكذب

  • Lying — Untruthfulness that can be joking without malice, commission lying, or omission lying.الكذب يشمل المزاح دون سوء نية، الكذب بالتكليف، والكذب بالإغفالKey Word: Three Forms
  • Commission lying — Creating a perception or belief by words that intentionally deceive the receiver. Includes puffery in advertising.الكذب بالتكليف: خلق تصور أو اعتقاد بكلمات تضلل عمداً. يشمل المبالغة الإعلانيةKey Word: Active Deception
  • Omission lying — Intentionally not informing others of differences, problems, safety warnings, or negative issues related to a product or company.الكذب بالإغفال: عدم الإبلاغ عمداً عن المشاكل أو تحذيرات السلامةKey Word: Silent Deception

4) Conflicts of Interest تضارب المصالح

  • Conflict of interest — When an individual must choose whether to advance his or her own interests, those of the organization, or those of some other group.تضارب المصالح: الاختيار بين مصلحة شخصية أو مؤسسية أو جماعيةKey Word: Competing Loyalties
  • Employees must be able to separate their private interests from their business dealings.يجب فصل المصالح الشخصية عن المعاملات التجارية

5) Bribery الرشوة

  • Bribery — Offering something (often money) to gain an illicit advantage from someone in authority.الرشوة: تقديم شيء (غالباً مال) لمنفعة غير مشروعة من صاحب سلطةKey Word: Illicit Advantage
  • Active bribery — The person who promises or gives the bribe commits the offense.الرشوة الفعلية: من يعد بها أو يقدمهاKey Word: Giver's Offense
  • Passive bribery — Offense committed by the official who receives the bribe.الرشوة غير المباشرة: المسؤول الذي يتلقىKey Word: Receiver's Offense
  • Facilitation payments — Payments to obtain or retain business; do not constitute bribery for US companies in some situations under FCPA.مدفوعات التسهيل: لا تُعتبر رشوة للشركات الأمريكية في بعض الحالاتKey Word: Legal in US (not UK)
Note: In the UK, facilitation payments are illegal. The US FCPA makes it illegal to bribe a foreign official.

6) Corporate Intelligence الاستخبارات المؤسسية

  • Corporate intelligence — Collection and analysis of information on markets, technologies, customers, competitors, and external trends.جمع وتحليل المعلومات عن الأسواق والتقنيات والعملاء والمنافسين
  • Three intelligence models: (1) Passive monitoring for early warning, (2) Tactical field support, (3) Support dedicated to top-management strategy.ثلاثة نماذج: مراقبة سلبية، دعم ميداني تكتيكي، دعم استراتيجية الإدارة العلياKey Word: Three Models

7) Discrimination التمييز

  • Discrimination — Prejudices based on race, color, religion, sex, marital status, public assistance status, disability, age, national origin, or veteran status; illegal in the United States.التمييز غير قانوني في الولايات المتحدة
  • EEOC — Equal Employment Opportunity Commission; federal agency protecting against workplace discrimination.لجنة تكافؤ فرص العمل الفيدراليةKey Word: EEOC
  • Age Discrimination in Employment Act — Outlaws hiring practices that discriminate against people 40 or older, or require retirement before age 70.يحظر التمييز ضد من عمرهم 40+ أو التقاعد الإجباري قبل 70
  • Affirmative action — Efforts to recruit, hire, train, and promote qualified individuals from groups historically discriminated against.برامج العمل الإيجابي

8) Sexual Harassment التحرش الجنسي

  • Sexual harassment — Any repeated, unwanted behavior of a sexual nature perpetrated upon one individual by another.سلوك جنسي متكرر وغير مرغوب
  • Hostile work environment — 3 criteria:
    1. The conduct was unwelcome.غير مرغوب فيه
    2. The conduct was severe, pervasive, and regarded as hostile/offensive enough to alter employment conditions.شديد ومتكرر ويغير ظروف العمل
    3. The conduct is such that a reasonable person would find it hostile or offensive.الشخص العادي يراه عدائياً
    Key Word: Three-Part Test
  • Dual relationship — A personal/loving/sexual relationship with someone with whom you share professional responsibilities.العلاقة المزدوجة

9) Fraud الاحتيال

  • Fraud — Any purposeful communication that deceives, manipulates, or conceals facts in order to harm others.تواصل متعمد لخداع الآخرين أو إخفاء الحقائق
  • Accounting fraud — Inaccurate information in corporate financial reports that investors rely on.الاحتيال المحاسبي: معلومات غير دقيقة في التقارير المالية
  • Marketing fraud — Dishonestly creating, distributing, promoting, and pricing products.الاحتيال التسويقي
Form / الشكلDefinition / التعريف
PufferyExaggerated advertising upon which no reasonable buyer would rely.مبالغة إعلانية لا يعتمد عليها مشتر عاقل
Implied FalsityA message that tends to mislead, confuse, or deceive the public.رسالة مضللة أو مربكة
Literally FalseAdvertising claims "tests prove" (establishment) or makes bald assertions (non-establishment) that cannot be substantiated.كذب صريح
Ambiguous StatementsVague statements open to multiple interpretations.عبارات غامضة

10) Consumer Fraud احتيال المستهلك

  • Consumer fraud — When consumers attempt to deceive businesses for their own gain.محاولة المستهلك خداع الشركة لمكسب شخصي
  • Examples: price tag switching, item switching, lying for discounts, abusing return policies.تبديل الأسعار، استبدال المنتجات، الكذب للخصومات، استغلال الإرجاع
  • Collusion — An employee assists the consumer in fraud.التواطؤ
  • Duplicity — Staging an accident, wardrobing (wearing and returning), false defect claims.الخداع
  • Guile — Using tricks to gain an unfair advantage while understanding right/wrong.المكر
TypeDefinition
Chargeback FraudDisputing a legitimate transaction after receiving goods.الاعتراض على معاملة مشروعة بعد استلام البضائع
Price ArbitrageSwapping differently priced but similar items for a higher return.استبدال سلع بأسعار مختلفة
Return FraudDefrauding via the return process (e.g., returning rocks in a PlayStation box).احتيال الإرجاع
ShowroomingExamining products in-store then buying online cheaper.الفحص في المتجر والشراء أونلاين
WardrobingBuying an item, wearing it, then returning for refund.ارتداء الملابس ثم إرجاعها

11) Financial Misconduct المخالفات المالية

  • The failure to understand and manage ethical risks played a significant role in the global financial crisis (Great Recession).الفشل في إدارة المخاطر الأخلاقية لعب دوراً في الأزمة المالية العالمية
  • Subprime lending emerged as an ethics issue — loan officers got commissions with no consequences for defaults. "Liar loans" inflated sales.قروض الرهن العقاري عالية المخاطر وقروض الاحتيال
  • Risk management in the financial industry remains a key concern.إدارة المخاطر تبقى هاجساً

12) Insider Trading التداول بمعلومات داخلية

  • Insider trading — Buying or selling stocks by insiders who possess information that is not yet public. Illegal.شراء/بيع أسهم بمعلومات غير عامة — غير قانوني
  • Insider trading is legal if the individual legally trades stock in their own company AND reports to the SEC within two business days.قانوني إذا أبلغ SEC خلال يومي عملKey Word: 2-Day SEC Rule

The Challenge of Determining an Ethical Issue تحديد القضية الأخلاقية

  • Most ethical issues become visible through stakeholder concerns.تتجلى من خلال مخاوف أصحاب المصلحة
  • Problems can become ethical issues as a result of changing societal values.تتحول نتيجة تغير القيم المجتمعية
  • Crisis management — The process of handling a high-impact event characterized by ambiguity and the need for swift action.إدارة الأزمات: التعامل مع حدث بالغ التأثير بسرعة ودقة

Chapter 6 — A Framework for Ethical Decision Making in Business

الفصل السادس — إطار فهم اتخاذ القرار الأخلاقي في الأعمال

Source: Ferrell, Business Ethics 13e · Ch. 6

🎯 Key Takeaways — أهم النقاط (ملخّص سريع)
  • First step in ethical decision-making: Recognize that an ethical issue exists.الخطوة الأولى: التعرف على وجود قضية أخلاقية
  • Ethical Issue Intensity = importance to individual/group/org. Moral Intensity = perceived social pressure & harm.حدة القضية vs الحدة الأخلاقية
  • Individual Factors: Gender, Education, Nationality, Age, Locus of Control.5 عوامل فردية
  • Locus of Control: Internal = control own fate · External = go with flow.تحكم داخلي/خارجي
  • Organizational Factors: Corporate Culture · Ethical Culture · Significant Others · Obedience to Authority.عوامل تنظيمية
  • Significant Others (peers, managers) have MORE daily impact than CEO, religion, or education.الزملاء أكثر تأثيراً يومياً من الرئيس أو الدين
  • Opportunity = conditions that permit unethical behavior. Absence of punishment = opportunity.الفرصة = غياب العقاب
  • Guilt is the first sign of an unethical decision.الذنب = أول علامة على قرار غير أخلاقي
  • Normative = how decisions SHOULD be made. Descriptive = how they ACTUALLY are.المعياري vs الوصفي
  • Rawls' Veil of Ignorance: design principles without knowing your future position → fairness.حجاب الجهل: صمّم المجتمع وأنت لا تعرف موقعك
  • Equality Principle = basic rights compatible with others. Difference Principle = inequalities must benefit the least-advantaged.مبدأ المساواة ومبدأ الفرق
  • Core Values are the blueprint for ethical decisions and stakeholder priorities.القيم الأساسية = خارطة طريق

6-1 Ethical Issue Intensity حدة القضية الأخلاقية

  • Ethical awareness — The ability to perceive whether a situation or decision has an ethical dimension.الوعي الأخلاقي: القدرة على إدراك البُعد الأخلاقي للموقف أو القرارKey Word: Perceive Ethical Dimension
  • Ethical issue intensity — The relevance or importance of an event or decision in the eyes of the individual, work group, and/or organization.حدة القضية الأخلاقية: مدى أهمية الحدث في نظر الفرد أو الفريق أو المنظمةKey Word: Perceived Importance
  • Personal and temporal in character — influenced by values, beliefs, needs, perceptions, situational characteristics, and personal pressures.ذات طابع شخصي وزمني
  • Moral intensity — Individuals' perceptions of social pressure and the harm they believe their decisions will have on others.الحدة الأخلاقية: تصور الفرد للضغط الاجتماعي وحجم الضررKey Word: Social Pressure + Harm
Note: Ethical issues may not reach critical awareness if managers fail to identify and educate employees about specific problem areas. Organizations with diverse employees must train workers on how the firm wants ethical issues handled.

6-2 Individual Factors العوامل الفردية

  1. Gender — Research shows in many aspects there are no differences between men and women in ethical decision making.الجنس: لا توجد فروق كبيرة في كثير من الجوانب
  2. Education — A significant factor; more education or work experience generally improves ethical decision making.التعليم: كلما زاد، تحسّنت القرارات الأخلاقيةKey Word: More Ed = Better Decisions
  3. Nationality — The legal relationship between a person and the country in which they are born.الجنسية
  4. Age — A complex relationship with business ethics; younger managers are more influenced by organizational culture.العمر: المديرون الأصغر أكثر تأثراً بثقافة المنظمة
  5. Locus of control — Individual differences about how one is affected by internal vs external events.مركز التحكم
    • External — See themselves as going with the flow; life events due to uncontrollable forces.التحكم الخارجي
    • Internal — Believe they control events through their own effort and skill; masters of their destinies.التحكم الداخلي
    Key Word: Internal vs External

6-3 Organizational Factors العوامل التنظيمية

  • Corporate culture — A set of values, norms, and artifacts, including ways of solving problems that employees share.ثقافة الشركة: قيم ومعايير وممارسات مشتركةKey Word: Shared Values
  • Ethical culture — Acceptable behavior, defined by the company and industry. Reflects the integrity of decisions made.الثقافة الأخلاقية: السلوك المقبول كما تحدده الشركة والقطاع
  • Ethical culture is shaped by: (1) corporate policies, (2) top management's leadership on ethical issues, (3) influence of coworkers, (4) opportunity for unethical behavior.أربعة عوامل تشكلهاKey Word: Four Shaping Factors
  • Significant others — Those who have influence in a work group: peers, managers, coworkers, subordinates.الأشخاص المؤثرون في فريق العمل
  • Obedience to authority — Employees resolve ethics issues by simply following the directives of a superior.الطاعة للسلطةKey Word: Follow the Boss

6-4 Opportunity الفرصة

  • Opportunity — The conditions in an organization that limit or permit ethical or unethical behavior.الظروف التي تحد أو تسمح بالسلوك غير الأخلاقيKey Word: Conditions for Behavior
  • Immediate job context — Where individuals work, whom they work with, and the nature of the work.سياق العمل المباشر
  • Can be deterred with formal codes, policies, and rules adequately enforced by management.يُردع بالمدونات والسياسات والقواعد المُطبَّقة
  • Misconduct can still occur without proper oversight. Opportunity also comes from knowledge and exploiting knowledge.قد تنشأ من المعرفة واستغلالها
Note: An example of failing to erect barriers against unethical behavior is a company policy that does NOT punish employees who accept large gifts from clients.

6-5 Business Ethics Intentions, Behavior, and Evaluations نوايا وسلوك وتقييمات أخلاقيات الأعمال

  • Ethical business issues and dilemmas involve problem-solving situations where rules are often vague or in conflict.قواعد غامضة أو متعارضة
  • When intentions and behavior are inconsistent with their ethical judgment, people may feel guilty — next step: change behavior to reduce the feeling.الشعور بالذنب يدفع لتغيير السلوك
  • The road to success depends on how the businessperson defines success. Success drives intentions and behavior either implicitly or explicitly.تعريف النجاح يوجّه النوايا والسلوك

6-6 Using the Ethical Decision-Making Model استخدام نموذج اتخاذ القرار الأخلاقي

  • The model does NOT determine if a decision is right or wrong — it provides insights about how ethical decisions are made.لا يحدد الصواب/الخطأ بل يقدم رؤىKey Word: Insights, Not Verdicts
  • Business ethics involves value judgments and collective agreement about acceptable patterns of behavior.أحكام قيمة واتفاق جماعي
  • Understanding the factors helps differentiate between an ethical issue and a dilemma.التمييز بين القضية والمعضلة

6-7 Normative Considerations in Ethical Decision Making الاعتبارات المعيارية

  • Normative approaches — How organizational decision makers SHOULD approach an issue. Different from descriptive approaches that examine how they actually do.المقاربات المعيارية: ما ينبغي أن يكون، لا ما هو كائنKey Word: Should vs Does
  • Most organizations develop a set of core values to provide enduring beliefs about appropriate conduct.القيم الأساسية
  • Instrumental concern — Focuses on positive outcomes: firm profitability and benefits to society.الاهتمام بالنتائج الإيجابية

Institutions as the Foundation for Normative Values المؤسسات كأساس للقيم المعيارية

  • Institutional theory — Organizations operate according to taken-for-granted institutional norms and rules.النظرية المؤسسية: معايير مقبولة دون جدال
  • Government, religion, and education are key institutions influencing the creation of values, norms, and conventions.الحكومة والدين والتعليمKey Word: Three Institutions
  • Industry competition determined by: (a) barriers to entry, (b) available substitutes, (c) power of rivals over customers, (d) power of rivals' suppliers.محددات المنافسة الصناعية

Implementing Principles and Core Values تطبيق المبادئ والقيم الأساسية

  • Veil of ignorance (Rawls) — A thought experiment examining how individuals would formulate principles if they did NOT know their future position in society.حجاب الجهل: صياغة المبادئ دون معرفة موقعك المستقبليKey Word: Rawls Thought Experiment
  • Equality principle — Each person has basic rights compatible with the basic liberties of others.مبدأ المساواة
  • Difference principle — Economic/social inequalities should be arranged to provide the most benefit to the least-advantaged.مبدأ الفرق: أكبر فائدة للأقل حظاًKey Word: Benefit Least-Advantaged
  • Companies convert principles into core values: sustainability, collaboration, teamwork, avoiding bribery.تحويل المبادئ إلى قيم جوهرية

Understanding Ethical Decision Making فهم اتخاذ القرار الأخلاقي

  • Top-level support for ethical behavior is instrumental in helping employees.دعم القيادة العليا أساسي
  • Normative perspectives set forth ideal goals to which organizations should aspire.أهداف مثالية
  • Knowledge about ethical decision making helps in making good decisions.المعرفة تؤدي لقرارات سليمة

Chapter 7 — Moral Philosophy and Cognitive Moral Development

الفصل السابع — الفلسفة الأخلاقية وتطور الأخلاق الإدراكي

Source: Ferrell, Business Ethics 13e · Ch. 7

🎯 Key Takeaways — أهم النقاط (ملخّص سريع)
  • Moral Philosophy = personal principles (individual). Business Ethics = group decisions.الفلسفة شخصية، أخلاقيات الأعمال جماعية
  • Teleology (Consequentialism) = judged by results. Includes Egoism & Utilitarianism.الغائية = النتائج
  • Deontology (Nonconsequentialism) = rights & intentions matter. Kant's Categorical Imperative.الواجبية = النوايا والحقوق
  • Utilitarianism: greatest good for the greatest number. Rule vs Act.النفعية: أعظم خير لأكبر عدد
  • Egoism: maximize self-interest. Enlightened Egoism: long-term, considers others but self stays #1.الأنانية vs الأنانية المستنيرة
  • Relativist Perspective: ethics subjective; derived from group consensus.النسبية = إجماع المجموعة
  • Virtue Ethics: what a person of good character would do. Applied inductively.أخلاق الفضيلة: استقرائية
  • 3 Types of Justice: Distributive (outcomes), Procedural (processes), Interactional (relationships).3 أنواع عدالة
  • Kohlberg's 6 Stages of Cognitive Moral Development — 3 levels: Pre-conventional · Conventional · Post-conventional.6 مراحل كولبرغ، 3 مستويات
  • Adam Smith: business guided by morals of good people. Milton Friedman: profit is the goal, market regulates itself.سميث vs فريدمان
  • White-Collar Crime: nonviolent crimes by educated individuals in positions of power. Monetary loss exceeds violent crime combined.جرائم الياقات البيضاء
  • Categorical Imperative (Kant): "Act so your rule could become a universal law."الواجب المطلق لكانط
  • A personal moral compass is NOT sufficient — need ethical-reasoning training + oversight.البوصلة الشخصية غير كافية

7-1 Moral Philosophy Defined تعريف الفلسفة الأخلاقية

  • Moral philosophy — The specific principles or values people use to decide what is right and wrong.المبادئ والقيم المحددة لتحديد الصواب والخطأKey Word: Right vs Wrong Principles
  • Determines how conflicts in human interests are settled and optimizes mutual benefit of people living in groups.حل التعارض وتعظيم المنفعة المتبادلة
  • Often used to defend a particular economic system (e.g., capitalism) and individuals' behavior within it.تُستخدم للدفاع عن النظام الاقتصادي
  • Adam Smith — Father of free-market capitalism; believed business should be guided by the morals of good people.آدم سميث: الأب الفكري للرأسمالية
  • Milton Friedman — Supported profit maximization as the ultimate goal; argued the market will reward or punish companies without government regulation.فريدمان: تعظيم الأرباح هو الهدف
  • Economic freedom — Based on self-ownership, right to choose, voluntary exchange, open markets, and defined property rights.الحرية الاقتصادية
Note: Moral philosophies are person-specific, while business ethics is based on decisions made by groups or when carrying out tasks to meet business objectives.

Key Orientations التوجهات الرئيسية

  • Economic value orientation — Values quantified by monetary means; if an act produces more economic value than effort, it's ethical.التوجه نحو القيمة الاقتصادية
  • Idealism — Moral philosophy placing special value on ideas and ideals as products of the mind.المثالية: الأفكار نتاج العقل
  • Realism — The view that an external world exists independent of our perceptions.الواقعية: العالم الخارجي موجود مستقلاً

7-2 Comparison of Moral Philosophies مقارنة الفلسفات الأخلاقية

Philosophy / الفلسفةDescription / الوصف
Teleology مبدأ الغايةActs are right if they produce a desired result (self-interest, utility).الأفعال صحيحة إذا أنتجت نتيجة مرغوبة
Egoism الأنانيةRight actions maximize the individual's self-interest as defined by the individual.تعظيم مصلحة الفرد
Utilitarianism النفعيةRight actions maximize total utility — the greatest good for the greatest number.أكبر خير لأكبر عدد

Instrumental vs Intrinsic Goodness الخير الأداتي والخير الجوهري

  • Monists — Believe only one thing is intrinsically good.الموحدون: شيء واحد فقط خير مطلق
  • Hedonism — Pleasure is the ultimate good; the best moral end is the greatest balance of pleasure over pain.مذهب المتعة
    • Quantitative hedonists — More pleasure is better.كمي: المزيد أفضل
    • Qualitative hedonists — One can get too much of a good thing.نوعي: الإفراط ممكن
  • Pluralists (nonhedonists) — No single thing is intrinsically good. Plato: the good life is a mixture of moderation, beauty, wisdom, sciences/arts, and pure pleasures of the soul.التعدديون
  • Instrumentalists — Reject that ends can be separated from means or that outcomes are intrinsically good. A more modern view.أصحاب المذهب الأدائي
  • Goodness theories — Focus on the end result and the goodness/happiness created.نظريات الخير
  • Obligation theories — Emphasize means and motives; divided into teleology and deontology.نظريات الواجب

Teleology مبدأ الغاية

  • Teleology — Acts are morally right if they produce some desired result (pleasure, knowledge, self-interest, utility, wealth, fame).الأفعال صحيحة إذا أنتجت نتيجة مرغوبة
  • Consequentialism — Teleological philosophies that assess moral worth by looking at consequences.العواقبية
  • Egoism — Right behavior is defined by consequences for the individual.الأنانية الأخلاقية
  • Enlightened egoism — Long-range perspective allowing for well-being of others, but self-interest remains paramount.الأنانية المستنيرةKey Word: Long-Range Self-Interest
  • Utilitarianism — Greatest good for the greatest number.النفعية
    • Rule utilitarians — Follow general rules to decide the best action.قائمة على القواعد
    • Act utilitarians — Evaluate each individual action for its utility.قائمة على الفعل

Deontology الواجبية

  • Deontology — Focuses on the rights of individuals and the intentions associated with behavior rather than consequences.حقوق الأفراد ونواياهم
  • Nonconsequentialism — Certain behaviors are inherently right; focuses on the individual actor, not society.اللاعواقبية
  • Kant's Categorical Imperative — If you'd feel comfortable letting everyone see you commit an act AND your rationale could become a universal principle, the act is ethical.الواجب المطلق لكانطKey Word: Universalizability
  • Rule deontologists — Conformity to general moral principles based on logic determines ethicalness.قائمون على القواعد
  • Act deontologists — Actions are the proper basis to judge morality.قائمون على الأفعال
Teleology vs Deontology:
Teleology — Ends justify the means; what's good for the person/community/society/world.
Deontology — Inalienable rights; some things are inherently right/wrong.

Relativist Perspective وجهة النظر النسبية

  • Relativist perspective — Definitions of ethical behavior derive subjectively from individual and group experiences.تعريفات ذاتية من التجارب
  • Descriptive relativism — Relates to observations of other cultures.الوصفية
  • Meta-ethical relativism — People see situations from their own perspectives; no objective way to resolve disputes between value systems.الميتا-أخلاقية
  • Normative relativism — Assumes one person's opinion is as good as another's.المعيارية
Problem: Relativism emphasizes people's differences while ignoring their basic similarities. Studies suggest relativism is negatively correlated with sensitivity to ethical issues.

Virtue Ethics and Justice أخلاق الفضيلة والعدالة

  • Virtue ethics — Ethical behavior involves not only adhering to conventional moral standards but also considering what a mature person with a "good" moral character would deem appropriate.أخلاق الفضيلة
  • Justice — Fair treatment and due reward in accordance with ethical or legal standards.العدالة
    • Distributive justice — Based on evaluation of outcomes/results of a business relationship.التوزيعية: النتائج
    • Procedural justice — Considers the processes and activities producing an outcome.الإجرائية: العمليات
    • Interactional justice — Based on relationships between organizational members.التفاعلية: العلاقات
    Key Word: Three Types of Justice
Q: What's the difference between deontology, teleology, and virtue ethics?
A: The first two are applied deductively to problems; virtue ethics is applied inductively.

Applying Moral Philosophy to Decision Making تطبيق الفلسفة الأخلاقية

  • Individuals use different moral philosophies depending on whether it's a personal or work decision.تختلف بين الشخصي والمهني
  • Goals and pressures differ in personal vs professional lives. Factors are weighted differently in a business (profit) situation. Corporate culture has influence.الثقافة المؤسسية تؤثر
  • The concept of a moral philosophy is inexact — must be assessed on a continuum, not as static entities.متصل وليس ثابتاً

Kohlberg's Model of Cognitive Moral Development نموذج كولبرغ لتطور الأخلاق الإدراكي

People make different decisions in similar ethical situations because they are in different moral development stages.يتخذ الناس قرارات مختلفة لأنهم في مراحل مختلفة

Stage / المرحلةDescription / الوصف
1. Punishment & Obedienceالعقاب والطاعةRight/wrong tied to a person with power, not higher philosophy.مرتبط بصاحب السلطة
2. Individual Instrumental Purpose & Exchangeالغاية الفردية والتبادلRight is what serves individual needs; behavior evaluated by its fairness to the self.ما يخدم احتياجات الفرد
3. Mutual Interpersonal Expectations, Relationships & Conformityالتوقعات المتبادلةEmphasizes interests of others, though motivation still comes from obedience to rules.مصالح الآخرين والامتثال
4. Social System & Conscience Maintenanceالنظام الاجتماعي والضميرRight = duty to society. Duty, respect for authority, maintenance of social order are focal.الواجب تجاه المجتمع
5. Prior Rights, Social Contract, or Utilityالحقوق السابقة والعقد الاجتماعيUpholds basic rights, values, legal contracts. Recognizes legal and moral views may conflict.قد تتعارض القانونية والأخلاقية
6. Universal Ethical Principlesالمبادئ الأخلاقية العالميةRight determined by universal ethical principles. People have inalienable rights valid by universality.مبادئ عالمية وحقوق غير قابلة للتصرف

White-Collar Crime جرائم ذوي الياقات البيضاء

  • White-collar crime — Crimes perpetrated every year by nonviolent business criminals.جرائم غير عنيفة يرتكبها رجال أعمال
  • Causes more damage in monetary and emotional loss in one year than violent crimes over several years combined.خسائر أكبر من جرائم العنف
  • Offenders tend to be highly educated, in positions of power, trust, respectability, and responsibility.متعلمون في مناصب ثقة
  • Corporate culture can transcend individual beliefs — patterns become institutionalized, sometimes encouraging unethical behaviors.الثقافة المؤسسية تتجاوز المعتقدات الفردية
  • Undecided employees tend to go along with coworkers; some may have inherently criminal personalities.غير الحاسمين ينساقون

Individual Factors in Business Ethics العوامل الفردية

  • Individual moral decisions are much more complex than learning about different moral philosophies.أكثر تعقيداً من معرفة الفلسفات
  • A personal moral compass is important but NOT sufficient to prevent ethical misconduct in an organizational context.البوصلة الشخصية مهمة لكنها غير كافية
  • Developing strong abilities in ethical reasoning likely leads to more ethical decisions than individualized character education.تطوير قدرات التفكير الأخلاقي

Quick Review

All key definitions and concepts at a glance.

Chapter 5 — Key Definitions

Ethical Issue
A problem, situation, or opportunity that requires a choice among actions that must be evaluated as right or wrong.
Ethical Dilemma
A problem requiring a choice among actions that all have negative outcomes — no right choice, only less-unethical ones.
Collusion
A secret agreement between two or more parties for a fraudulent, illegal, or deceitful purpose.
Integrity
One of the most important elements of virtue — being whole, sound, and unimpaired in decisions and culture.
Fairness — 3 Elements
Equality (fair distribution), Reciprocity (give-and-take), Optimization (equity vs. efficiency trade-off).
Commission Lying
Creating a perception by words that intentionally deceive — includes advertising puffery.
Omission Lying
Intentionally failing to inform others of defects, safety warnings, or negative product issues.
Conflict of Interest
When an individual must choose between advancing personal, organizational, or another group's interests.
Active vs Passive Bribery
Active = promising/giving the bribe. Passive = the official who receives it.
Facilitation Payments
Small payments to obtain/retain business. Legal for US companies in some FCPA situations; illegal in the UK.
EEOC
Equal Employment Opportunity Commission — federal agency protecting against workplace discrimination.
Hostile Work Environment — 3 Criteria
(1) Unwelcome conduct, (2) severe and pervasive, (3) a reasonable person would find it hostile.
Puffery
Exaggerated advertising on which no reasonable buyer would rely.
Insider Trading
Trading stock on non-public info — illegal. Legal only if in one's own company AND reported to the SEC within 2 business days.
Crisis Management
Handling a high-impact event marked by ambiguity and the need for swift action.

Chapter 6 — Key Definitions

Ethical Awareness
The ability to perceive whether a situation or decision has an ethical dimension.
Ethical Issue Intensity
The relevance or importance of an event in the eyes of the individual, group, or organization. Personal and temporal.
Moral Intensity
Individuals' perceptions of social pressure and the harm their decisions will have on others.
Locus of Control
Internal = believe you control events through effort. External = events due to uncontrollable forces.
Corporate Culture
A set of values, norms, and artifacts — ways of solving problems — that employees share.
Ethical Culture — 4 Factors
(1) Corporate policies, (2) top management's leadership, (3) coworker influence, (4) opportunity for unethical behavior.
Significant Others
Peers, managers, coworkers, and subordinates who influence a work group's ethical decisions.
Obedience to Authority
When employees resolve ethical issues by simply following a superior's directives.
Opportunity
Conditions in an organization that limit or permit ethical/unethical behavior. Deterred by enforced codes and policies.
Immediate Job Context
Where individuals work, whom they work with, and the nature of the work itself.
Normative vs Descriptive
Normative = how decision makers SHOULD approach issues. Descriptive = how they actually do.
Institutional Theory
Organizations operate by taken-for-granted institutional norms. Three key institutions: government, religion, education.
Instrumental Concern
Focuses on positive outcomes — firm profitability and benefits to society.
Veil of Ignorance (Rawls)
Thought experiment: form principles without knowing your future position in society.
Difference Principle
Economic/social inequalities should provide the most benefit to the least-advantaged.

Chapter 7 — Key Definitions

Moral Philosophy
Specific principles or values people use to decide what is right and wrong. Person-specific.
